What is Fake News?

Fake news is false or misleading information presented as real news to confuse or mislead people. that is deliberately created and spread to deceive, manipulate, or harm, often masquerading as legitimate news to gain financial or political influence. It often aims to incite strong emotional responses, such as fear or anger, which helps it spread quickly across social media and the internet.

Types of Fake News

  • Satire or Parody: Humorous content not intended to harm but can be misinterpreted as true.
  • False Connection: Headlines, visuals, or captions that don't match the content, often seen in clickbait.
  • Misleading Content: Using information to frame an issue or person by twisting facts.
  • False Context: Genuine content shared with inaccurate background information
  • Imposter Content: Impersonating real sources like legitimate news websites.
  • Manipulated Content: Altered genuine information, such as edited photos or videos.
  • Fabricated : Entirely false content created to deceive and cause harm.

How to Identify Fake News

  • Check trusted sources
  • Verify from official websites
  • Read full article
  • Check author and date
  • Do not share without confirmation
